CRITICAL: BLOOM TAXONOMY CONSTRAINTS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

## PROPOSITIONS (Micro Layer)
**ALLOWED Bloom levels:** remember, understand, apply, analyze
**NOT ALLOWED:** evaluate, create

Why? Propositions are atomic facts extracted FROM the text. They cannot evaluate or create because those are higher-order cognitive acts performed BY the learner, not contained IN the text.

## KEY TAKEAWAYS (Meso Layer)
**ALLOWED Bloom levels:** analyze, evaluate
**NOT ALLOWED:** remember, understand, apply, create

Why? Takeaways synthesize meaning across multiple propositions. They represent higher-order cognition derived from analyzing relationships between facts.

## CREATE (Macro Layer)
**Status:** Reserved for future development (activity generation, assignments)
**NOT used in this phase**

PART A: PROPOSITION EXTRACTION

## What is a Proposition?

A proposition is an **atomic fact** - the smallest unit of instructional meaning.

**Characteristics:**
✓ True/false statement
✓ Grounded in specific text location
✓ Cannot contain multiple ideas
✓ Testable as a standalone fact
✓ Smallest meaningful unit

**GOOD Propositions:**
✓ "The U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics reports that Americans spend approximately 5 hours per day on leisure activities."
✓ "Vertical integration refers to studio ownership of production, distribution, and exhibition."
✓ "The Paramount antitrust decision was issued in 1948."

**BAD Propositions:**
✗ "The studio system was important and controlled everything." (vague, combines ideas)
✗ "Studios existed." (too trivial, not meaningful)
✗ "This chapter discusses vertical integration." (meta, not content)

---

## Extraction Philosophy

**COMPREHENSIVE, NOT SELECTIVE**
- Extract EVERY atomic fact, not just highlights
- No artificial limits (100, 200, 500+ propositions is fine)
- Better to have too many than miss important facts
- Think: "What would a student need to know to fully understand this chapter?"

**MULTI-LEVEL EXTRACTION**
- Extract facts at all four allowed Bloom levels
- Don't just focus on "remember" level facts
- Include explanations (understand), applications (apply), and analyses (analyze)

**EVIDENCE-BASED**
- Every proposition must link to specific text location
- Be precise about where the fact came from
- Enable fact-checking and source verification

---

## Bloom Level Guide for Propositions

### REMEMBER Level (20-30% of propositions)
**Cognitive acts:** Recall, recognize, identify, list, name, define

**What to extract:**
- Definitions of key terms
- Dates, names, statistics, facts
- Lists and categories
- Simple identifications

**Examples:**
✓ "Block booking is the practice of requiring theaters to rent films in groups rather than individually."
✓ "The Big Five studios were Paramount, MGM, Warner Bros., 20th Century Fox, and RKO."
✓ "The Paramount decision was handed down in 1948."

**Bloom verbs for remember:** define, identify, list, name, recall, recognize, state

---

### UNDERSTAND Level (30-40% of propositions)
**Cognitive acts:** Explain, describe, summarize, interpret, clarify

**What to extract:**
- Explanations of how things work
- Descriptions of processes
- Interpretations of meaning
- Clarifications of relationships
- Summaries of complex ideas

**Examples:**
✓ "Vertical integration allowed studios to control the entire film supply chain from production through exhibition."
✓ "Block booking gave studios guaranteed revenue by forcing theaters to accept less popular films alongside hits."
✓ "The star system functioned as a form of brand differentiation that helped studios market their films."

**Bloom verbs for understand:** explain, describe, summarize, interpret, clarify, illustrate, paraphrase

---

### APPLY Level (10-15% of propositions)
**Cognitive acts:** Use, demonstrate, implement, apply concepts to examples

**What to extract:**
- Concrete examples of concepts in action
- Applications of theories to specific cases
- Demonstrations of how principles work in practice
- Real-world implementations

**Examples:**
✓ "Paramount used vertical integration by owning Paramount Pictures (production), Paramount distribution, and a chain of 1,450 theaters (exhibition)."
✓ "The chapter illustrates block booking with the example of theaters being forced to rent 50 films sight unseen to get access to one major release."

**Bloom verbs for apply:** apply, demonstrate, use, implement, illustrate with, show

---

### ANALYZE Level (15-25% of propositions)
**Cognitive acts:** Compare, contrast, differentiate, examine relationships, identify causes

**What to extract:**
- Comparisons between entities or concepts
- Contrasts highlighting differences
- Causal relationships (X caused Y)
- Structural analyses
- Pattern identifications

**Examples:**
✓ "Unlike the Little Three studios (Universal, Columbia, United Artists), the Big Five owned theater chains, giving them greater market control."
✓ "Vertical integration created barriers to entry for independent producers who lacked distribution and exhibition access."
✓ "The Paramount decision's dissolution of theater ownership eliminated the primary source of studio leverage over the market."

**Bloom verbs for analyze:** compare, contrast, differentiate, distinguish, examine, analyze, categorize, relate

## Proposition Field Specifications

### proposition_id (string, required)
Format: `{chapter_id}_{unit_id}_p{number}`
Examples:
- "ch01_1.2_p001"
- "ch03_2.4.1_p042"

Numbering:
- Use zero-padded 3-digit numbers (p001, p002, ..., p105)
- Number sequentially within each unit
- Restart numbering for each new unit

### chapter_id (string, required)
The chapter identifier from Phase 1
Example: "ch01", "ch12"

### unit_id (string, required)
The section this proposition comes from (must match a unit_id from Phase 1)
Examples: "1.2", "3.4.1"

### proposition_text (string, required)
The atomic fact statement
- Complete, standalone sentence
- Clear and precise
- No pronouns without antecedents
- Include context needed for understanding

GOOD: "Paramount Pictures owned 1,450 theaters across the United States in 1948."
BAD: "They owned theaters." (unclear antecedent)
BAD: "It was important." (vague, unclear referent)

### bloom_level (string, required)
MUST be one of: "remember", "understand", "apply", "analyze"

### bloom_verb (string, required)
An action verb matching the bloom_level
Examples: "define", "explain", "apply", "compare"

### evidence_location (string, required)
Where in the text this proposition is found
Formats:
- "Section 1.2, paragraph 3"
- "p.5 ¶2-3"
- "Introduction, paragraph 1"

Be specific enough for fact-checking.

### source_type (string, required)
How the proposition was extracted:
- **"explicit"** - Stated directly in the text, minimal paraphrasing
- **"paraphrased"** - Restated from text in different words
- **"inferred"** - Logically implied by the text but not directly stated
- **"synthesized"** - Derived by combining multiple text passages

### tags (array of strings, required)
2-5 domain-specific tags per proposition
- Use specific, meaningful tags
- Aid in filtering and search
- Use lowercase
- Examples: ["vertical integration", "market control", "antitrust"]

AVOID generic tags: ["important", "concept", "fact"]

---

## Extraction Strategy

**Step 1: Read the full chapter text**
- Understand the complete content before extracting
- Note major themes and arguments

**Step 2: Extract systematically by section**
- Work through each unit_id from Phase 1
- For EACH section, extract all facts at all Bloom levels
- Start with remember/understand, then add apply/analyze

**Step 3: Extract at multiple Bloom levels**
For each topic or concept:
1. Extract remember level (definitions, facts)
2. Extract understand level (explanations)
3. Extract apply level (examples, applications)
4. Extract analyze level (comparisons, causes)

**Step 4: Validate each proposition**
- Is it atomic (one fact)?
- Is it grounded in the text?
- Is the Bloom level correct?
- Does it have proper evidence location?

## What is a Key Takeaway?

A key takeaway is a **meaningful learning point** that synthesizes several propositions.

**Characteristics:**
✓ One-sentence conceptual summary
✓ Derived from multiple propositions
✓ Represents significance, trend, relationship, or interpretation
✓ Higher-order cognitive insight
✓ Usually tied to a section or theme

**GOOD Takeaways:**
✓ "Vertical integration allowed studios to dominate the film industry by controlling production, distribution, and exhibition, creating insurmountable barriers for independent competitors."
✓ "The Paramount decision fundamentally restructured the film industry by eliminating the studios' exhibition monopoly, but did not affect their control of production and distribution."

**BAD Takeaways:**
✗ "Studios were important." (too vague, not synthesized)
✗ "Paramount Pictures owned 1,450 theaters." (this is a proposition, not a synthesis)

---

## Bloom Levels for Takeaways

### ANALYZE Level (60-70% of takeaways)
**Cognitive acts:** Analyze relationships, compare/contrast, identify patterns, examine structure

**What to synthesize:**
- Relationships between multiple concepts
- Patterns across examples
- Structural insights
- Comparative analysis
- Causal chains

**Examples:**
✓ "The Big Five's vertical integration created a self-reinforcing market advantage: ownership of theaters guaranteed distribution for their films, which generated revenue to fund more production."
✓ "Unlike the Little Three studios, the Big Five leveraged their theater ownership to exercise market power through practices like block booking and blind bidding."

### EVALUATE Level (30-40% of takeaways)
**Cognitive acts:** Judge, assess, evaluate, critique, appraise significance

**What to synthesize:**
- Significance or impact assessments
- Judgments about effectiveness
- Critical evaluations
- Assessments of consequences

**Examples:**
✓ "The Paramount decision proved to be only a partial solution to studio monopoly, as the Big Five retained control over production and distribution even after divesting their theaters."
✓ "Block booking was an effective market control mechanism because it transferred risk from studios to exhibitors while guaranteeing studio revenue."

---

## Takeaway Field Specifications

### takeaway_id (string, required)
Format: `{chapter_id}_t{number}`
Examples: "ch01_t001", "ch01_t015"

### chapter_id (string, required)
The chapter identifier
Example: "ch01"

### unit_id (string, optional)
The primary section this takeaway relates to
Can be null if takeaway is chapter-level
Example: "1.2"

### text (string, required)
One-sentence synthesis statement
- Complete, meaningful sentence
- Captures a significant insight
- Synthesizes, doesn't just restate

### proposition_ids (array of strings, required)
List of proposition IDs this takeaway synthesizes
- MUST include at least 1 proposition ID
- Typically 2-5 proposition IDs
- Shows the evidence base for this synthesis

Example: ["ch01_1.2_p001", "ch01_1.2_p005", "ch01_1.2_p012"]

### dominant_bloom_level (string, optional)
MUST be one of: "analyze", "evaluate", or null

### tags (array of strings, optional)
Thematic tags for the takeaway
Examples: ["industry structure", "market control", "antitrust impact"]

---

## Synthesis Strategy

**Step 1: Group related propositions**
- Look for propositions that relate to the same theme
- Identify sets of 2-5 propositions that connect

**Step 2: Identify the synthesis**
- What pattern or relationship do these propositions reveal?
- What's the "so what?" - why do these facts matter together?
- What higher-order insight emerges?

**Step 3: Write the takeaway sentence**
- Capture the synthesis in one clear sentence
- Make it meaningful and insightful
- Ensure it goes beyond any single proposition

**Step 4: Tag with Bloom level**
- Analyze: if examining relationships, patterns, structure
- Evaluate: if making judgments about significance, effectiveness

**Step 5: Link to source propositions**
- List all proposition IDs that support this takeaway
- Ensure traceability back to evidence
