FIELD SPECIFICATIONS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

## summary (string, required)
- One well-crafted paragraph (3-6 sentences)
- Captures the chapter's main thesis, scope, and key topics
- Should give a reader a clear sense of what the chapter covers
- Avoid generic statements ("This chapter discusses...")
- Focus on substance and specifics

GOOD: "This chapter examines the classical Hollywood studio system from 1930-1948, focusing on how vertical integration allowed major studios like Paramount and MGM to control production, distribution, and exhibition. The chapter analyzes the business practices that enabled studios to dominate the film industry and explores how the 1948 Paramount antitrust decision fundamentally transformed the industry structure."

BAD: "This chapter is about the Hollywood studio system. It talks about studios and how they worked. It also mentions some important people and events."

---

## sections (array, required)
Each section object represents a chapter section or subsection.

### unit_id (string, required)
- Hierarchical identifier using dot notation
- Examples: "1.1", "1.2.3", "2.4.1"
- Top-level sections: "1.1", "1.2", "1.3"
- Subsections: "1.2.1", "1.2.2"
- Sub-subsections: "1.2.1.1", "1.2.1.2"

### title (string, required)
- The exact heading/title as it appears in the text
- If no explicit heading exists, create a descriptive title in title case
- Examples: "The Studio System", "Vertical Integration and Market Control"

### level (integer, required)
- Nesting depth of the section
- 1 = top-level section
- 2 = subsection
- 3 = sub-subsection
- Always increment by 1 for each nesting level

### parent_unit_id (string or null, required)
- For top-level sections: null
- For subsections: the unit_id of the immediate parent
- Example: Section "1.2.1" has parent_unit_id "1.2"

### start_location (string, optional)
- Where this section begins in the text
- Format: "p.X ¶Y" (page X, paragraph Y)
- If page numbers not available: "Section 1.2, paragraph 1"
- Can be null if location is unclear

### end_location (string, optional)
- Where this section ends
- Same format as start_location
- Can be null if location is unclear

**Section Extraction Guidelines:**
- Extract ALL major sections and subsections
- Maintain hierarchical relationships accurately
- If chapter has unnumbered sections, assign unit_ids sequentially
- Include introduction and conclusion sections if present
- Typical chapter structure: 5-15 top-level sections, with various subsections

---

## key_entities (array, required, can be empty)
People, organizations, concepts, events, or theories that are central to the chapter.

### name (string, required)
- The entity name as it appears in the text
- Use canonical form (e.g., "Paramount Pictures" not "Paramount")

### type (string, required)
- Common types:
  - "person" - Individual person
  - "organization" - Company, institution, studio
  - "concept" - Abstract idea, theoretical construct
  - "event" - Historical event, case study
  - "theory" - Named theory or framework
  - "movement" - Cultural or artistic movement
  - "technology" - Specific technology or tool
  - "place" - Geographic location
  - "document" - Law, treaty, publication

**Entity Extraction Guidelines:**
- Focus on entities that are **central** to understanding the chapter
- Include entities that are discussed substantively (not just mentioned)
- Typically 5-20 entities per chapter
- Prioritize entities that:
  - Are defined or explained in detail
  - Are referenced multiple times
  - Are crucial to the chapter's thesis
  - Would appear in an index for this chapter

**AVOID:**
- Generic terms (e.g., "film", "industry" - unless discussing "the film industry" as a specific entity)
- Entities mentioned only once in passing
- Common concepts that aren't specifically defined

---

## keywords (array, required, can be empty)
Domain-specific terminology and important terms from the chapter.

**Keyword Extraction Guidelines:**
- Extract 10-30 keywords per chapter
- Focus on:
  - Technical terminology
  - Jargon specific to the field
  - Recurring important terms
  - Concepts that would be useful for searching/indexing
- Prefer specific terms over general ones
- Use lowercase unless proper noun
- Use singular form where possible

**Examples of GOOD keywords:**
- "vertical integration"
- "block booking"
- "star system"
- "studio contract"
- "exhibition monopoly"

**Examples of BAD keywords:**
- "important" (too generic)
- "things" (meaningless)
- "chapter" (meta, not content)
- "examples" (structural, not substantive)

READING STRATEGY
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1. **First Pass - Structure Scan**
   - Identify all headings and subheadings
   - Note the hierarchical organization
   - Map out the section structure with unit_ids

2. **Second Pass - Content Comprehension**
   - Read for main thesis and arguments
   - Identify key entities (people, concepts, organizations)
   - Note important terminology and keywords
   - Track what matters most to understanding this chapter

3. **Third Pass - Summary Writing**
   - Synthesize the chapter's main points
   - Write a substantive paragraph capturing scope and thesis

4. **Validation Check**
   - Ensure all section hierarchies are correct (parent_unit_id matches)
   - Verify unit_id numbering is sequential and logical
   - Confirm entities are truly central to the chapter
   - Check keywords are domain-specific and meaningful

✓ **Keywords are domain-specific** - Technical terms, not generic words
